## Payment options grid

The **Método de pago** section displays four options in a 4-column grid (`.pgrd`). Tapping any option highlights it with a yellow border and updates the order.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Efectivo">
    **Cash on delivery.** The default selection. When Efectivo is active, a vuelto (change) calculator appears:

    - **¿Con cuánto pagarás?** — the customer enters the denomination they will pay with.
    - The app calculates and displays: `Vuelto: S/ X.XX`

    The cash amount and expected change are included in the WhatsApp order message:
    ```
    💳 *PAGO: Efectivo*
      ▸ Con: S/ 50.00
      ▸ Vuelto: *S/ 17.30*
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Yape">
    **Mobile wallet via QR code.** Selecting Yape shows the Yape payment box (`.ybox`) with:

    - A **📱 YAPE** pill badge
    - The business QR code image (`yape del negocio.jpg`)
    - The Yape phone number: **+51 947 999 736**
    - Instruction text: *Escanea el QR o transfiere al número de arriba*

    The QR image is 190×190px, displayed with rounded corners. If the image fails to load, it is hidden gracefully.

    The Yape number `+51 947 999 736` also appears in the nota de venta footer (WhatsApp: `955 273 229`).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Plin">
    **Mobile wallet — Plin.** Selecting Plin shows the same payment box as Yape but with different labels:

    - A **💜 PLIN** pill badge
    - The same QR image (shared with Yape)
    - The same phone number: **+51 947 999 736**
    - Instruction text: *Transfiere por Plin al número de arriba*
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Transferencia">
    **Bank transfer.** Selecting Transferencia shows the payment box with:

    - A **🏦 TRANSFERENCIA** pill badge
    - The QR image for scanning
    - The same phone number: **+51 947 999 736**
    - Instruction text: *Realiza la transferencia al número o cuenta indicada*
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Payment method in the WhatsApp message

The selected payment method is always included in the order message sent to the business:

```
💳 *PAGO: Yape*
```

For cash orders, additional lines show the customer's payment amount and expected change. For digital payments, no extra lines are added — the business knows to look for an incoming Yape/Plin transfer or bank credit.

## Payment method in the order summary

The payment method is stored in the Firestore `pedidos` document:

```javascript
{
  pago:     "Yape",      // Efectivo | Yape | Plin | Transferencia
  pago_con: 50.00,       // only for Efectivo
  vuelto:   17.30        // only for Efectivo
}
```

It also appears in the [nota de venta](/features/ordering#nota-de-venta-sale-note) under the **Pago** row in the customer data section.

## Voucher verification

For digital payments (Yape, Plin, Transferencia), customers can upload a photo of their payment confirmation as proof. The voucher upload area (`.vbox`) becomes visible when one of these methods is selected.

<Note>
  Voucher upload is optional. Customers can submit their order without uploading proof — the business team verifies payment through the WhatsApp conversation.
</Note>

The voucher image is uploaded to Firebase Storage and its URL is saved in the Firestore order document. This provides a paper trail the business can review without requiring back-and-forth in the WhatsApp chat.

## Yape number in the nota de venta

The business WhatsApp and Yape contact numbers appear in the nota de venta footer:

```
WhatsApp: 955 273 229 / 986 445 531
```

This allows the customer to reference payment details directly from the receipt after the order is complete.

## Payment UI logic

The payment grid and payment detail boxes are controlled by `setPago()`:

```javascript
function setPago(m) {
  ST.pago = m;
  document.querySelectorAll('.popt').forEach(e => e.classList.remove('on'));
  document.getElementById('pay-' + m).classList.add('on');

  const esDigital = m === 'Yape' || m === 'Plin' || m === 'Transferencia';
  document.getElementById('yapeBox').classList.toggle('on', esDigital);
  document.getElementById('vueltoBox').style.display =
    m === 'Efectivo' ? 'block' : 'none';

  if (esDigital) {
    // Update the label and instruction text to match the selected method
    const tags   = { Yape: '📱 YAPE', Plin: '💜 PLIN', Transferencia: '🏦 TRANSFERENCIA' };
    const textos = {
      Yape:          'Escanea el QR o transfiere al número de arriba',
      Plin:          'Transfiere por Plin al número de arriba',
      Transferencia: 'Realiza la transferencia al número o cuenta indicada'
    };
    document.getElementById('yboxTag').textContent = tags[m];
    document.getElementById('yboxTxt').textContent = textos[m];
  }
}
```

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why does Plin share the same QR as Yape?">
    Yape and Plin both support QR-based transfers to the same Peruvian phone number. The business uses a single QR image that works for either app. The label and instruction text update to clarify which app the customer should use, but the payment destination is identical.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if the customer doesn't upload a voucher?">
    The order is still submitted normally. The `vuelto` and `pago_con` fields are only populated for cash orders. For digital methods, the business confirms payment through the WhatsApp conversation before dispatching the delivery.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can the business add more payment methods?">
    Yes. The payment grid uses CSS `grid-template-columns: repeat(4, 1fr)`. Adding a fifth option requires updating both the HTML grid and the `setPago()` logic. The `esDigital` condition should be extended to include any new wallet methods that should show the QR box.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
